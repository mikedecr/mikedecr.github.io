Gender Gap: what’s the Democratic share of the two party vote among women, vs. the Democratic share of the two-party vote among men? \[\begin{align} \frac{x_{w}}{n_{w}} - \frac{x_{m}}{n{m}} \end{align}\]

Conventionally: take \(x_{g}\) divided by \(n_{g}\), treat the resulting proportion as a random variable, and create a confidence interval assuming a fixed \(n_{g}\) and random \(x_{g}\).

Problem: \(n_{g}\) is not fixed. It is a random variable that itself is subject to sampling variability. This is more transparent if we write in probability notation: \[\begin{align} \text{Gender Gap} &= p(\text{Dem} \mid \text{Woman}) - p(\text{Dem} \mid \text{Woman}) \\[12pt] \text{Gender Gap} &= \frac{p(\text{Dem} \cap \text{Woman})}{p(\text{Woman})} - \frac{p(\text{Dem} \cap \text{Man})}{p(\text{Man})} \end{align}\]
