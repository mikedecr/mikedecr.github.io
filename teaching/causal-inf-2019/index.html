Causal Inference Reading Group

In 2019 I organized and led discussion for a summer reading group on causal inference methods. Much of the material focused on “the view from political science,” but we regularly incorporated material from statistics, economics, and epidemiology. We discussed potential outcomes and graphical models for causal inference and estimation methods using regression, propensity score adjustment, and structural models.

The reading list below contains both the assigned and the “bonus” readings.

Readings

Introduction

  • Rubin (1974), “Estimating Causal Effects of Treatments in Randomized and Nonrandomized Studies”
  • LaLonde (1986), “Evaluating the Econometric Evaluations of Training Programs with Experimental Data”
  • McElreath (2019), “Statistical Rethinking 2019” Lecture 6 (DAGs)
  • Aronow and Samii (2016), “Does Regression Produce Representative Estimates of Causal Effects?"

Causal Identification

  • Holland (1986), “Statistics and Causal Inference”
  • Kang and Schafer (2007), “Demystifying Double Robustness: A Comparison of Alternative Strategies for Estimating a Population Mean from Incomplete Data”
  • Sekhon (2007), “The Neyman-Rubin Model of Causal Inference and Estimation via Matching Methods”
  • Stuart (2010), “Matching methods for causal inference: A review and a look forward”
  • Bowers, Fredrickson, and Panagopoulos (2013), “Reasoning about Interference Between Units: A General Framework”
  • Hartman, Grieve, Ramsahai, and Sekhon (2015), “From sample average treatment effect to population average treatment effect on the treated: combining experimental with observational studies to estimate population treatment effects”
  • Keele (2015), “The statistics of causal inference: A view from political methodology”
  • Hartman and Hidalgo (2018), “An equivalence approach to balance and placebo tests”

Causal Mediation

  • Imai, Keele, Tingley, and Yamamoto (2010), “Unpacking the black box of causality: Learning about causal mechanisms from experimental and observational studies”
  • VanderWeele and Vansteelandt (2015), “Mediation Analysis with Multiple Mediators”
  • Myers and Tingley (2016), “The Influence of Emotion on Trust”

Causal Heterogeneity

  • Kam and Trussler (2017) “At the Nexus of Observational and Experimental Research: Theory, Specification, and Analysis of Experiments with Heterogeneous Treatment Effects”
  • Coppock, Leeper, and Mullinix (2017), “Generalizability of heterogeneous treatment effect estimates across samples”
  • Meager (2019), “Understanding the Average Impact of Microcredit Expansions: A Bayesian Hierarchical Analysis of Seven Randomized Experiments”

Null Effects and Statistical Power

  • Mayo (2004), “An error-statistical philosophy of evidence”
  • Broockman (2014), “Do female politicians empower women to vote or run for office? A regression discontinuity approach”
  • Gelman and Carlin (2014), “Beyond Power Calculations: Assessing Type S (Sign) and Type M (Magnitude) Errors”
  • Bhavnani (2017), “Do the Effects of Temporary Ethnic Group Quotas Persist? Evidence from India”
  • Coppenolle (2017), “Political Dynasties in the UK House of Commons: The Null Effect of Narrow Electoral Selection”
  • Loken and Gelman (2017), “Measurement error and the replication crisis”
  • Fowler (2019), “But Shouldn’t That Work Against Me?"

G-Methods

  • Naimi, Cole, and Kennedy (2017), “An introduction to g methods”
  • Acharya, Blackwell, and Sen (2016), “Explaining Causal Findings Without Bias: Detecting and Assessing Direct Effects”

DAGs (1)

  • Greenland, Pearl, Robins (1999), “Causal Diagrams for Epidemiological Research”
  • Keele, Stevenson, and Elwert (2019) “The causal interpretation of estimated associations in regression models”

DAGs (2)

  • VanderWeele and Robins (2014) “Signed directed acyclic graphs for causal inference”
  • Mohan and Pearl (2014), “Graphical Models for Recovering Probabilistic and Causal Queries from Missing Data”
  • Imbens (2019), “Potential Outcome and Directed Acyclic Graph Approaches to Causality: Relevance for Empirical Practice in Economics”
  • Lattimore and Rohde (2019), “Replacing the do-calculus with Bayes rule”

Machine Learning

  • Hastie, Tibshirani, and Friedman (2009) “The Elements of Statistical Learning Data Mining, Inference, and Prediction (2 ed.)"
  • Hill (2011), “Bayesian Nonparametric Modeling for Causal Inference”
  • Samii, Paler, and Daly (2016), “Retrospective causal inference with machine learning ensembles: An application to anti-recidivism policies in Colombia”
  • Wager and Athey (2018), “Estimation and Inference of Heterogeneous Treatment Effects using Random Forests”
  • Abraham and Sun (2019), “Estimating Dynamic Treatment Effects in Event Studies with Heterogeneous Treatment Effects”
  • Cheng, Khomtchouk, Matloff, and Mohanty (2019), “Polynomial Regression as an Alternative to Neural Nets”
  • Hahn, Murray, and Carvalho (2019), “Bayesian Regression Tree Models for Causal Inference: Regularization, Confounding, and Heterogeneous Effects”
  • McElreath (2019), “Statistical Rethinking 2019” Lecture 19 (Gaussian Processes)

Regression Discontinuity

  • Calonico, Cattaneo, and Titiunik (2014), “Robust Nonparametric Confidence Intervals for Regression-discontinuity Designs”
  • Caughey and Sekhon (2014), “Elections and the regression discontinuity design: Lessons from close US house races, 1942–2008”
  • Skovron and Titiunik (2015), “A Practical Guide to Regression Discontinuity Designs in Political Science”
  • Imbens and Wager (2019), “Optimized Regression Discontinuity Designs”

Time-Series Cross-Sectional and Panel Data

  • Blackwell and Glynn (2018), “How to Make Causal Inferences with Time-Series Cross-Sectional Data under Selection on Observables”
  • Goodman-Bacon (2018), “Difference-in-Differences with Variation in Treatment Timing”
  • Torres (2019), “Estimating Controlled Direct Effects Through Marginal Structural Models”
