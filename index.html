Michael DeCrescenzo

Ph.D. Candidate, Political Science

University of Wisconsin–Madison

I am a PhD student in political science (quantitative methods & U.S. politics) at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

My work develops Bayesian approaches for measurement and causal inference, including applications with machine learning methods. I use these tools to study U.S. public opinion, voting, and election administration. My work is published in the Election Law Journal and The Forum, and it has been covered by the New York Times, the Washington Post, FiveThirtyEight, and more.

I intend to finish my Ph.D. in late summer 2020 and am on the market for (non-academic) positions in quantitative research or data science. Feel free to view my resume or my academic CV.
