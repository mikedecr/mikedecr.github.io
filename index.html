Michael DeCrescenzo

Ph.D. Candidate, Political Science

University of Wisconsin–Madison

I study U.S. elections and public opinion with Bayesian methods.

My current projects deal with ideological representation in primary elections, party coalitions, and election administration. I have growing interests in model-driven measurement approaches and Bayesian approaches to causal inference.

I employ computational methods throughout my work, most notably with statistical analysis (MCMC methods) and open-source project development.

I am an affiliate and research fellow of the Elections Research Center and an Honored Instructor at UW–Madison.
